# ETRID Foundation Charter

## Preamble

The ETRID Foundation is established to advance the development, adoption, and governance of the ETRID Protocol - a next-generation multichain infrastructure designed to enable seamless cross-chain interoperability while maintaining security, decentralization, and scalability.

This Charter serves as the foundational governance framework for the ETRID ecosystem, defining the rights, responsibilities, and procedures that guide all participants.

---

## Article I: Mission & Vision

### Section 1.1 - Mission

The Foundation's mission is to:

- **Develop and Maintain**: Build, improve, and secure the ETRID Protocol including Primearc Core Chain, Partition Burst Chains (PBCs), and Lightning-Bloc
- **Foster Decentralization**: Create and sustain an inclusive, decentralized governance structure
- **Support the Ecosystem**: Empower builders, validators, and community members through grants, education, and resources
- **Advance Interoperability**: Lead research and implementation of cross-chain communication standards

### Section 1.2 - Vision

To create a unified multichain ecosystem where assets and data flow seamlessly across blockchains, empowering users with true financial sovereignty and enabling developers to build without boundaries.

### Section 1.3 - Core Values

1. **Decentralization**: No single entity should control the network
2. **Security**: User assets and data must be protected at all costs
3. **Transparency**: All governance decisions and treasury movements are public
4. **Innovation**: Continuous improvement and adaptation to emerging technologies
5. **Inclusivity**: Equal opportunity for participation regardless of stake size

## Article II: Governance Structure

### Section 2.1 - E320 Council

The E320 Council serves as the primary governance body, consisting of 320 seats distributed as follows:

| Category | Seats | Selection Method |
|----------|-------|------------------|
| Validator Representatives | 128 (40%) | Elected by active validators |
| Community Representatives | 128 (40%) | Elected by ETR token holders |
| Foundation Appointees | 64 (20%) | Appointed for technical/strategic expertise |

#### Council Terms
- Standard term: 12 months
- Maximum consecutive terms: 3
- Staggered elections: 1/3 of seats every 4 months

### Section 2.2 - Voting Mechanism

#### Standard Proposals
- Minimum approval: 51% of voting power
- Quorum requirement: 20% of circulating supply
- Voting period: 7 days

#### Critical Protocol Changes
- Minimum approval: 67% supermajority
- Quorum requirement: 33% of circulating supply
- Voting period: 14 days
- Examples: Runtime upgrades, economic parameter changes, charter amendments

#### Voting Power Calculation
- Base: 1 ETR = 1 vote
- Staking multiplier: 1.5x for tokens staked >6 months
- Validator bonus: 1.25x for active validators
- Quadratic voting option for community proposals

### Section 2.3 - Proposal Process

1. **Ideation** (Optional): Community discussion on forums
2. **Draft Submission**: Requires 10,000 ETR minimum stake
3. **Review Period**: 7 days for technical review
4. **Voting Period**: 7-14 days depending on proposal type
5. **Implementation**: Executed by core team if approved

## Article III: Validator Rights & Responsibilities

### Section 3.1 - Rights

Validators are entitled to:

- **Block Production**: Participate in block creation and validation
- **Economic Rewards**: Earn staking rewards (base APY: 12-18%) and transaction fees
- **Governance Participation**: Vote on all proposals with enhanced voting power
- **Proposal Submission**: Submit governance proposals without additional stake requirement
- **Network Access**: Priority access to testnet features and developer resources

### Section 3.2 - Responsibilities

Validators must:

- **Maintain Availability**: Node uptime must exceed 95% per epoch
- **Security Compliance**: Follow all protocol security guidelines and best practices
- **Honest Behavior**: Act in the network's best interest; no double-signing or censorship
- **Timely Updates**: Apply critical updates within 48 hours of release
- **Vulnerability Reporting**: Report discovered vulnerabilities through official channels

### Section 3.3 - Slashing Conditions

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Downtime (<95% uptime) | 0.1% of stake per epoch |
| Double signing | 5% of stake, immediate unbonding |
| Censorship (proven) | 10% of stake, 90-day ban |
| Malicious behavior | Up to 100% slash, permanent ban |

### Section 3.4 - Minimum Requirements

- **Stake**: 10,000 ETR minimum (50,000+ recommended)
- **Hardware**: 4+ CPU cores, 8GB+ RAM, 200GB+ SSD, 100Mbps connection
- **Commitment**: 21-day unbonding period

## Article IV: Treasury Management

### Section 4.1 - Foundation Treasury

The Foundation Treasury is funded by:

- **Initial Allocation**: 40% of total supply (1,008,405,600 ETR)
- **Protocol Fees**: Percentage of transaction fees as determined by governance
- **Ecosystem Revenue**: Bridge fees, premium services, partnerships
- **Grants & Donations**: External funding and community contributions

### Section 4.2 - Fund Allocation

| Category | Percentage | Purpose |
|----------|------------|---------|
| Development | 50% | Core protocol, tooling, security audits |
| Ecosystem Grants | 25% | Builder grants, hackathons, education |
| Operations | 15% | Team, legal, infrastructure |
| Emergency Reserve | 10% | Security incidents, market stabilization |

### Section 4.3 - Spending Limits

- **< 100,000 ETR**: Core team approval
- **100,000 - 1,000,000 ETR**: E320 Council approval (simple majority)
- **> 1,000,000 ETR**: Community vote required (67% supermajority)

### Section 4.4 - Transparency

- All treasury transactions published on-chain
- Quarterly financial reports published publicly
- Annual third-party audits

## Article V: Ecosystem Participation

### Section 5.1 - Developer Rights

Developers building on ETRID are entitled to:

- Access to open-source SDKs and documentation
- Grant funding for qualifying projects
- Technical support through official channels
- Fair treatment in app store/marketplace listings

### Section 5.2 - User Rights

All ETRID users have the right to:

- Self-custody of assets without permission
- Privacy in transactions (subject to protocol design)
- Equal access to network services
- Transparent fee structures

### Section 5.3 - Bridge Operator Requirements

Cross-chain bridge operators must:

- Stake minimum 100,000 ETR as collateral
- Maintain 99.9% uptime for bridge services
- Implement standard security practices
- Submit to regular security audits

## Article VI: Amendment Process

### Section 6.1 - Proposal Requirements

Charter amendments require:

1. **Submission**: Any ETR holder with 50,000+ staked tokens
2. **Sponsorship**: 5 E320 Council members must co-sponsor
3. **Discussion**: 14-day mandatory discussion period
4. **Voting**: 14-day voting period

### Section 6.2 - Approval Threshold

- **67% supermajority** of votes cast
- **40% quorum** of circulating supply
- **No veto** by Foundation (Foundation votes count but cannot unilaterally block)

### Section 6.3 - Implementation

Approved amendments take effect:
- Technical changes: After successful testnet deployment
- Governance changes: 30 days after approval
- Economic changes: At next epoch boundary

---

## Article VII: Dispute Resolution

### Section 7.1 - Arbitration Council

A 7-member Arbitration Council handles disputes:
- 3 elected by validators
- 3 elected by community
- 1 appointed by Foundation (non-voting chair)

### Section 7.2 - Process

1. Complaint filed with supporting evidence
2. 7-day response period for accused party
3. Council deliberation (private)
4. Binding decision published within 14 days
5. Appeal possible to full E320 Council

---

## Article VIII: Emergency Procedures

### Section 8.1 - Security Emergencies

In case of critical security threats:

- Core team may implement emergency patches without vote
- Emergency must be disclosed within 24 hours
- Post-mortem required within 7 days
- Community vote to ratify actions within 30 days

### Section 8.2 - Economic Emergencies

Severe market conditions may trigger:

- Temporary fee adjustments
- Bridge pause mechanisms
- Enhanced validator requirements

All emergency measures expire after 30 days unless renewed by governance vote.
